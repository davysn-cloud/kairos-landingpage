# Joby:Abranddesignedtotakeflight

Sensory storytelling. Immersive design. An experience, elevated.

Watch Reel

The challenge

Joby Aviation had already created something extraordinary — all-electric air travel, designed for everyday life. What they needed was a brand as visionary as their technology.

So how do you elevate the act of flying into something that stirs wonder? You bring it back to the golden age, when taking to the skies still felt like a dream.

![](https://framerusercontent.com/images/TrZ09GjuCqotog7vl5deQH9HQ.jpg?width=1051&height=1381)

Envisioninganewera

The rebrand was driven by a strategic shift from product to brand, and from aircraft to experience. This evolution set the stage for Joby’s landmark U.S. launch in 2026. We reimagined the full ecosystem, spanning strategy and messaging, visual identity, digital experience, and more.

1.1

ColorSystem

A flexible color system bridges future and past. Core blues evoke open skies and optimism, while sun-washed oranges reflect Joby’s Californian roots. Supporting hues extend the system, allowing the brand to adapt across environments, footage, and moments while balancing heritage with possibility.

1.2

Typography

Custom-designed with Briton Smith at Family Type, the letterforms draw inspiration from the golden age of flight, reinterpreted for clarity, legibility, and motion across modern applications. Aerodynamic shapes and subtle historical references, including the distinctive “b” in Joby, nod to aviation’s heritage, while custom numerals and glyphs are engineered to perform wherever the brand lives.

1.3

Brandinaction

From app screens to billboards, a restrained system of color, typography, and flight-inspired forms translates Joby’s vision into real-world moments.

Conclusion

Joby’s rebrand marks monumental firsts: the first consumer-facing eVTOL brand identity and the first to design proprietary passenger experiences spanning aircraft, app, and skyport.

Together with Joby, we didn’t just design a brand. We helped define an entirely new category, guided by the belief that the sky was never the limit.
